Product Overview

Exceptional Portability and Response Capability

The FIM-92 Stinger is a Man-Portable Air-Defense System (MANPADS) designed for Short-Range Air Defense (SHORAD). Utilizing infrared homing technology, it boasts excellent tracking capabilities and its compact design allows for flexible deployment across various operational environments.

Key Features

Portable Design

A lightweight and rugged system designed for single-operator use, offering outstanding mobility for rapid deployment and movement at the infantry level.

Air Defense Role

Serves as a critical short-range air defense asset, protecting ground forces from threats approaching at low altitudes.

Field-Proven Legacy

Since its introduction in 1981, it has been operated by numerous nations worldwide, historically proving its reliability and durability.

Compact System Concept

Boasts an efficient space utilization and intuitive system configuration with an integrated design combining the launch tube, sight, and cooling unit.

History & Legacy

The history of the FIM-92 Stinger system began with the development of a next-generation air defense solution to replace existing man-portable anti-aircraft weapons. Through continuous sensor upgrades and advancements in guidance systems from its early models, the Stinger has evolved to maintain reliable performance even in the complex electronic warfare environments of modern battlefields.

This system has been integrated into the defense networks of the United States and various allied nations worldwide. Its operational scope has expanded beyond ground-launched variants to include platforms mounted on helicopters and light tactical vehicles. Alongside its technological progress, the Stinger is recognized as a monumental piece of equipment that set the standard for infantry anti-aircraft weapons.
